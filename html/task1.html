sir:magdi Yacoub

Sir Magdi Habib Yacoub OM FRS (Arabic: د/مجدى حبيب يعقوب
[ˈmæɡdi ħæˈbiːb jæʕˈʔuːb]; born 16 November 1935) is an Egyptian retired professor of cardiothoracic surgery at Imperial College London,
best known for his early work in repairing heart valves with surgeon Donald Ross, adapting the Ross procedure,where the diseased aortic valve
is replaced with the person's own pulmonary valve, devising the arterial switch operation (ASO) in transposition of the great arteries,
and establishing the heart transplantation centre at Harefield Hospital in 1980 with a heart transplant for Derrick Morris,
who at the time of his death was Europe's longest-surviving heart transplant recipient. Yacoub subsequently performed the UK's first combined heart and lung transplant
